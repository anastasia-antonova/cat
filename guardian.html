In Defense of Animals Works to Update the Way We Think About Animals

A huge focus of In Defense of Animals' mission and work is to promote animal rights and liberate all animals from the systemic cruelty and exploitation that continues to harm them.

Our Guardians Campaign works towards that goal by encouraging people to change the way we think and speak about animals. They are not property or commodities for us to own and exploit for utilitarian purposes.

Animals are sentient beings deserving of compassion, understanding, respect and rights.

Though this campaign we're bridging the gap between us and them, and reconstructing the social and legal relationships that exist between us.

SIGN THE GUARDIAN PLEDGE

Animals are our fellow beings, and deserve to be thought of as individuals with their own autonomy and interests.

They are not our property, we are not their owners.

Since the other animals we share the planet with should never be considered property or commodities, objects or things, I will refer to them as I refer to fellow humans — never as "it" — but by name, or as "he," "she," "who" or "they" as appropriate.

I pledge to always think and act as a guardian or protector, never as an owner or master of a fellow being.

As a guardian, I will rescue and adopt animals in need who will live as part of my family. When I have the opportunity, I will urge others to do the same.

Guardian Cities Are Stepping up for Animals

It may only be a simple change of phrase, but replacing the term "owner" with "guardian" is a powerful way to support animal rights. Our Guardians Campaign has worked with legislators at the city, county and state level across the U.S. to get them to legally acknowledge that animals are more than just mere property to be owned by including the term "guardian" in their animal-related laws and ordinances.
